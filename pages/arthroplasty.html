What is Joint Replacement?

Arthroplasty, commonly known as joint replacement surgery, is a procedure where a damaged joint is replaced with an artificial implant. This life-changing surgery can relieve pain, restore function, and significantly improve quality of life for patients with severe joint damage.

At Align Plus Ortho, we specialize in advanced joint replacement techniques using state-of-the-art implants and minimally invasive approaches that lead to faster recovery and better outcomes.

Restored Mobility

Regain ability to walk, climb stairs, and perform daily activities without pain.

Pain Relief

Significant reduction or complete elimination of chronic joint pain.

Improved Quality of Life

Return to hobbies, sports, and activities you enjoy with renewed confidence.

Long-Lasting Results

Modern implants are designed to last 15-20 years or more with proper care.

Types of Joint Replacement

Expert care for all major joint replacements

Total Knee Replacement (TKR)

Total knee replacement involves replacing all three compartments of the knee joint with artificial components made of metal and plastic. This procedure is ideal for patients with severe osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or post-traumatic arthritis affecting the entire knee.

Our Advanced Techniques:

  • Computer-Navigated Surgery: Enhanced precision with real-time imaging
  • Minimally Invasive Approach: Smaller incisions, faster recovery
  • Patient-Specific Implants: Customized based on your anatomy
  • Robotic-Assisted Surgery: Unparalleled accuracy and alignment
  • Rapid Recovery Protocols: Get home sooner, recover faster

Total Hip Replacement (THR)

Hip replacement involves replacing the damaged ball-and-socket joint of the hip with artificial components. Our surgeons use advanced approaches to minimize muscle damage and accelerate recovery while ensuring optimal implant positioning and longevity.

Our Advanced Techniques:

  • Direct Anterior Approach: Muscle-sparing technique with faster recovery
  • Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS): Multimodal pain management
  • Dual Mobility Implants: Reduced dislocation risk, increased stability
  • Ceramic-on-Ceramic Bearings: Excellent durability for younger patients
  • Same-Day Discharge Program: Return home the same day as surgery

Total Shoulder Replacement

Shoulder replacement surgery can relieve pain and restore function in shoulders severely damaged by arthritis or injury. We offer both anatomic and reverse shoulder replacements, choosing the optimal approach based on your specific condition and needs.

Our Advanced Techniques:

  • Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty: For patients with rotator cuff tears
  • Anatomic Shoulder Replacement: Preserves natural shoulder anatomy
  • Stemless Implants: Bone-preserving option for younger patients
  • 3D Planning & Navigation: Precise implant positioning
  • Custom Glenoid Components: For complex bone loss cases

Partial Joint Replacement

When arthritis affects only one part of a joint, partial replacement may be an option. This minimally invasive approach preserves healthy bone and tissue while replacing only the damaged portion, leading to faster recovery and more natural joint function.

Our Partial Replacement Options:

  • Unicompartmental Knee Replacement: For isolated knee arthritis
  • Hip Resurfacing: Bone-conserving alternative to total hip replacement
  • Hemiarthroplasty: Partial shoulder replacement
  • Patellofemoral Replacement: For isolated kneecap arthritis
  • Bipolar Hemiarthroplasty: For certain hip fracture cases

When is Joint Replacement Needed?

Recognizing the right time for surgery

Common Indications

Signs that joint replacement may be the right option

  • Severe joint pain that limits daily activities
  • Pain at rest, day and night
  • Chronic stiffness and reduced mobility
  • Inadequate pain relief from medications
  • Difficulty walking or climbing stairs
  • Joint deformity or swelling
  • Failed conservative treatments
  • Advanced arthritis on X-rays

Pre-Surgical Evaluation

Comprehensive assessment before joint replacement

  • Detailed medical history review
  • Complete physical examination
  • X-rays and advanced imaging (MRI/CT)
  • Preoperative blood tests and health screening
  • Cardiac and pulmonary evaluation if needed
  • Weight optimization counseling
  • Prehabilitation (pre-surgery therapy)
  • Discharge planning and home preparation

Joint Replacement Recovery Timeline

What to expect during your recovery journey

1

Hospital Stay (1-3 Days)

Immediate postoperative care with pain management, initial physical therapy, and mobility training. Most patients begin walking with assistance within hours of surgery. Discharge planning begins immediately with focus on safe return home.

2

Early Recovery (First 2 Weeks)

Focus on pain control, wound care, and beginning rehabilitation exercises. Gradual increase in walking distance with assistive devices. Important to follow precautions specific to your joint replacement. Most patients transition from walker to cane during this period.

3

Rehabilitation Phase (2-6 Weeks)

Intensive physical therapy to restore range of motion, build strength, and improve functional mobility. Gradual return to daily activities. Driving may resume (if left-sided surgery). Many patients return to sedentary work by 4-6 weeks.

4

Return to Activities (6 Weeks - 3 Months)

Continued strengthening and functional training. Gradual return to recreational activities, swimming, cycling, and light sports. Most restrictions are lifted. Significant improvement in pain and function compared to pre-surgery.

5

Full Recovery (3-6 Months)

Maximum improvement in strength and function achieved. Return to most sports and physical activities. Follow-up appointments to monitor progress. Focus shifts to maintaining joint health and preventing future problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about joint replacement surgery

How long do joint replacements last?

Modern joint replacements are designed to last 15-20 years or more. With advanced materials like ceramic and highly cross-linked polyethylene, many implants now last 20-25 years. Implant longevity depends on several factors including patient age, activity level, weight, and surgical technique. Regular follow-up and maintaining a healthy lifestyle can help maximize the life of your joint replacement.

What is the typical hospital stay for joint replacement?

Most joint replacement patients stay in the hospital for 1-3 days. With our enhanced recovery protocols, many patients are able to go home within 24-48 hours. For hip replacements using the direct anterior approach, some patients may even qualify for same-day discharge. The exact length of stay depends on your overall health, progress with physical therapy, and home support system.

When can I drive after joint replacement surgery?

For right-sided knee or hip replacement, driving is typically allowed after 4-6 weeks when you have adequate control and reaction time. For left-sided surgery, you may be able to drive as early as 2-3 weeks if you have an automatic transmission. Most importantly, you must be off narcotic pain medications and feel confident in your ability to control the vehicle safely.

What activities can I do after joint replacement?

After full recovery, most patients can return to low-impact activities like walking, swimming, cycling, golf, and doubles tennis. High-impact activities like running, jumping, and contact sports are generally discouraged as they can accelerate wear on the implant. Your surgeon will provide specific recommendations based on your joint, implant type, and activity goals.

Are there any permanent restrictions after joint replacement?

Most patients have few permanent restrictions. For hip replacement, you may need to avoid certain extreme positions to prevent dislocation. For knee replacement, deep kneeling may be uncomfortable. You'll receive specific precautions based on your surgery type. The goal of joint replacement is to restore function and allow you to return to an active lifestyle with minimal restrictions.

What is the success rate of joint replacement surgery?

Joint replacement surgery has a very high success rate. Approximately 90-95% of patients experience significant pain relief and improved function. The surgery has a satisfaction rate of over 90% at 10 years. Success depends on proper patient selection, surgical technique, implant choice, and postoperative rehabilitation. At Align Plus Ortho, our success rates exceed national averages due to our advanced techniques and comprehensive care approach.
