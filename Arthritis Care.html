Understanding Arthritis

Arthritis is a common condition that causes pain and inflammation in joints. At Align Plus Ortho, we provide comprehensive care for all types of arthritis, focusing on pain relief, improved function, and joint preservation.

Our approach combines evidence-based treatments with advanced joint preservation techniques to help you maintain an active lifestyle while managing your arthritis symptoms effectively.

Common Types of Arthritis We Treat:

Osteoarthritis (OA)

Wear-and-tear arthritis that occurs when the protective cartilage cushioning the ends of bones wears down over time, most commonly affecting knees, hips, and hands.

Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

An autoimmune disorder where the immune system attacks the joints, causing inflammation, pain, and potential joint deformity if left untreated.

Psoriatic Arthritis

A form of arthritis that affects some people with psoriasis, causing joint pain, stiffness, and swelling along with skin symptoms.

Gout

A form of inflammatory arthritis characterized by sudden, severe attacks of pain, swelling, redness, and tenderness in joints, often at the base of the big toe.

Symptoms & Diagnosis

Recognizing arthritis symptoms for early intervention

Common Symptoms

Early recognition leads to better outcomes

  • Joint pain and tenderness
  • Stiffness, especially in the morning
  • Swelling around joints
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Grating sensation or clicking sounds
  • Bone spurs around affected joints
  • Fatigue and general malaise (RA)
  • Redness and warmth around joints

Diagnostic Methods

Accurate diagnosis for targeted treatment

  • Comprehensive medical history
  • Physical examination of joints
  • X-rays to detect bone changes
  • MRI for soft tissue evaluation
  • Blood tests for inflammatory markers
  • Joint fluid analysis
  • Ultrasound for inflammation detection
  • Bone density scans (DEXA)

Comprehensive Treatment Options

Personalized arthritis care for every stage

Medication Management

Customized pain relievers and supplements for effective symptom control

Joint Injections

Hyaluronic acid injections (Viscosupplementation) for joint lubrication and regenerative injections

Physical Therapy

Customized exercise programs to improve joint flexibility, strengthen supporting muscles, and enhance functional ability.

Weight Management

Personalized nutrition and exercise plans to reduce stress on weight-bearing joints like knees and hips.

Assistive Devices

Custom braces, splints, canes, and shoe inserts to reduce joint stress and improve mobility.

Hot & Cold Therapy

Therapeutic use of heat and cold to reduce pain, decrease inflammation, and improve joint mobility.

Arthroscopic Surgery

Minimally invasive procedures to remove loose cartilage, inflamed synovial tissue, or bone spurs causing joint pain.

Osteotomy

Bone reshaping procedures to realign joints and redistribute weight away from damaged areas.

Cartilage Restoration

Advanced techniques like microfracture, mosaicplasty, and autologous chondrocyte implantation to repair damaged cartilage.

Partial Joint Replacement

Replacing only the damaged portion of the joint while preserving healthy bone and tissue.

Synovectomy

Surgical removal of inflamed synovial tissue in rheumatoid arthritis to reduce pain and prevent joint damage.

Joint Fusion (Arthrodesis)

Fusing bones together to eliminate painful motion in severely damaged joints not suitable for replacement.

PRP Therapy

Platelet-Rich Plasma injections using concentrated growth factors from your own blood to stimulate healing and reduce inflammation.

Stem Cell Therapy

Using your body's own stem cells to promote cartilage regeneration and reduce joint inflammation.

Shockwave Therapy

Extracorporeal shockwave treatment to stimulate healing in chronic arthritic conditions.

PEMF Therapy

Pulsed Electromagnetic Field therapy to reduce pain and inflammation while promoting tissue healing.

Prolotherapy

Injection therapy that stimulates the body's natural healing processes to strengthen ligaments and reduce joint instability.

Laser Therapy

Low-level laser treatment to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and accelerate tissue repair in arthritic joints.

Arthritis Prevention Tips

Proactive measures to maintain healthy joints throughout life

1

Maintain Healthy Weight

Every extra pound puts 4 pounds of pressure on your knees. Maintaining a healthy weight reduces stress on weight-bearing joints.

2

Stay Active Regularly

Regular exercise strengthens muscles that support joints and helps maintain joint flexibility and range of motion.

3

Protect Your Joints

Use proper techniques when lifting, maintain good posture, and avoid repetitive stress on the same joints.

4

Eat Joint-Healthy Foods

Incorporate omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and calcium-rich foods into your diet to support joint health.

5

Stay Hydrated

Cartilage is mostly water, so staying well-hydrated helps keep joints lubricated and functioning properly.

6

Quit Smoking

Smoking increases inflammation throughout the body and can accelerate cartilage damage in joints.

7

Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to joint pain and don't ignore persistent symptoms. Early intervention leads to better outcomes.

8

Regular Check-ups

Regular medical check-ups can help detect early signs of arthritis and other joint conditions before they progress.
